About Me

I was born on May 12, 1955. I was raised on a farm by my parents, Mona and Abraham Simpson. In the mid-1960s, while I was between nine and twelve years old, Mona went into hiding following a run-in with the law. I attended Springfield High School and fell in love with Marge Bouvier in 1974. Marge became pregnant with Bart in 1979, while I was working at a miniature golf course. We were wed in a small wedding chapel across the state line. From there we spent our wedding reception alone at a truck stop, and the remainder of our wedding night at Marge's parents' house. After failing to get a job at the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant, I left Marge to find a job by which I could support my family. I briefly worked at a taco restaurant called the Gulp n' Blow, until Marge found me and convinced me to return. As a result, I confronted Mr. Burns and secured a job at the Plant. Marge became pregnant with Lisa in 1981, shortly before we bought our first house. In 1985 and 1986, I saw brief success as the lead singer and songwriter for the barbershop quartet the Be Sharps, even winning a Grammy. During my time with the group, I was frequently absent from home, which put stress on my marriage. After the group broke up due to creative differences, I went back to Springfield to continue my old life. Sometime in the late 1980s, I and Marge carefully budgeted so I could have my dream job as a pin monkey in a bowling alley. Unfortunately for me, Marge became pregnant with Maggie shortly after I started my new job, and not being able to support my family, I went back to the Nuclear Plant.
